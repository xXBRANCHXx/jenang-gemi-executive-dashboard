1. Purpose

What the Accounting workspace is for

The Accounting workspace is the operational finance screen inside the Executive Dashboard. It is designed for daily cash control: entering money out, entering bills before they are paid, paying saved bills, recording transfers between accounts, and logging non-marketplace money in.

The screen is intentionally separate from marketplace sales and wallet automation. Marketplace orders and wallet payouts are already tracked elsewhere. Accounting is where the team records manual finance events that would otherwise be missing from cash control.

Important rule: Do not record Shopee, TikTok, or Tokopedia sales as Manual Money In. Marketplace sales are already counted from Orders and Wallet. Use Transfer when marketplace money moves from a wallet into a bank account.

Use Accounting for

Daily operator entries
  • Packaging purchases paid today.
  • Ad payments already charged.
  • Courier or fulfillment expenses.
  • Offline customer payments.
  • Owner injections and reimbursements.
Finance control
  • Supplier bills that are not paid yet.
  • Partial bill payments.
  • Bank to cash transfers.
  • Marketplace wallet to bank transfers.
  • Receipt and data-quality review.

4. Command Bar

Filtering, refreshing, and starting work

The command bar controls what period you are looking at and gives quick entry shortcuts.

Month and range controls

ControlUse it whenWhat happens

This MonthYou are entering or reviewing current-period activity.Uses the current month.
Last MonthYou are checking the previous close.Loads the previous calendar month.
YTDYou need year-to-date context.Loads data from January 1 through the selected period.

Action buttons

  • Refresh: reloads the latest Accounting data.
  • Expense: switches the form to Expense Paid.
  • Bill: switches the form to Bill Received.
  • Pay Bill: switches the form to pay an existing open bill.
  • Transfer: switches the form to internal movement between accounts.
  • Export: downloads the current period as CSV.
  • Settings: currently shows read-only seeded configuration information.

7. Entry Workflows

How to record each type of activity

Expense Paid

Use Expense Paid when money has already left the business. This is the most common daily entry mode.

1
Select Expense Paid.
2
Enter the payment date and amount.
3
Choose the account that paid the expense.
4
Choose category, vendor/payee, brand, and channel.
5
Attach receipt URL or set Receipt Status correctly.
6
Click Save or Save & Add.
Example: Cash Drawer paid Rp275.000 to a courier for fulfillment. Category = Logistics, Channel = Fulfillment, Receipt Status = Attached.

Bill Received

Use Bill Received when you receive a supplier invoice but have not fully paid it yet. This creates a bill in the Bills Queue.

1
Select Bill Received.
2
Enter Bill Date, Due Date, and Amount.
3
Enter the vendor and bill/invoice number.
4
Choose category, brand, and channel.
5
Attach invoice URL if available.
6
Click Save. The bill appears in Open Bills.

Pay Bill

Use Pay Bill when paying a bill that already exists in the Bills Queue. This reduces the outstanding amount and creates a payment transaction.

1
Select Pay Bill, or click Pay from the bill row.
2
Choose the bill. The amount fills from the outstanding amount when available.
3
Choose the account that paid the bill.
4
Adjust amount if it is a partial payment.
5
Add receipt/reference details and Save.
Do not enter a paid supplier invoice twice. If the invoice already exists as a bill, use Pay Bill. If you enter it as a new Expense Paid too, expenses and cash movement will be overstated.
8. Transfers and Money In

Recording movement without double-counting

Transfer

Use Transfer for wallet payout, bank-to-bank transfer, cash deposit, or any movement between accounts. Transfers do not count as income or expense.

1
Select Transfer.
2
Enter date and amount.
3
Choose From Account and To Account.
4
If there is a bank fee, enter Transfer Fee.
5
Save.
Marketplace payout example: Shopee Wallet -> BCA Operating for Rp12.750.000. This is a Transfer because the sale was already captured by Orders/Wallet.

Manual Money In

Use Manual Money In only for non-marketplace money. Examples include owner injection, offline customer payment, manual invoice payment, refund, reimbursement, loan received, or other non-marketplace income.

1
Select Money In.
2
Choose Income Type.
3
Choose Received Into Account.
4
Enter source/customer and category.
5
Save after confirming this is not marketplace revenue.

Do

  • Use Transfer for marketplace wallet payout.
  • Use Manual Money In for offline customer payment.
  • Use Owner Injection for owner capital added to bank.

Do not

  • Do not enter Shopee order revenue manually.
  • Do not use Manual Money In to clean up wallet balances.
  • Do not skip the source field.
9. Bills Queue

Managing unpaid and upcoming bills

Bill statuses

StatusMeaningAction
UnpaidNo payment recorded yet.Pay when scheduled.
Partially paidSome payment has been recorded but balance remains.Pay remaining outstanding amount.
OverdueDue date is past and outstanding remains.Escalate and resolve quickly.
PaidOutstanding amount is zero.No action required unless receipt/reference is missing.
VoidBill was cancelled with a reason.Keep for audit trail; do not reuse.

Bill row actions

  • Pay: switches the form into Pay Bill mode and selects that bill.
  • View: opens a detail drawer with raw bill fields.
  • Void: cancels the bill after you enter a reason. Use this only when the bill is invalid or duplicated.
10. Ledger

Reading manual transaction history

What to review in the ledger

  • Date: confirm it belongs to the selected period.
  • Type: expense, transfer, manual income, owner injection, refund, or draft.
  • Direction: money out, money in, or internal transfer.
  • Account: confirm the correct bank/cash/wallet account was used.
  • Vendor / Payee: confirm the source or recipient is meaningful.
  • Receipt: missing receipts should be resolved before closing.
  • Related Bill: bill payments should point to the bill number when possible.

Voiding a transaction

Voiding is used instead of deletion so the audit trail remains intact. Use Void only when an entry is wrong, duplicated, or should not count. Always enter a short reason that a reviewer can understand later.

11. Summary and Insights

Using reporting sections

Summary

The Summary section gives profit context and cash movement context for the selected period. It helps compare operational cash movement against broader sales and gross profit context.

Insights

Insights group spending by category, vendor, brand, or channel. Use the tabs to answer questions such as:

  • Which category consumed the most cash this month?
  • Which vendor drove the largest expense?
  • Which brand is carrying shared costs?
  • Which channel is generating the most operational spending?
12. Review Queue

Cleaning data before month close

The Review Queue identifies records that need cleanup. A clean Review count is a good signal that the period can be exported and sent for finance review.

IssueWhat it meansHow to resolve
Missing receiptReceipt Status is Missing or no receipt URL exists.Attach a link or mark Not required with a good reason.
Overdue billA bill is past due and still outstanding.Pay, schedule, investigate, or void if invalid.
Missing categoryEntry does not have a useful category.Edit/update with the correct category.
Marketplace income riskManual Money In looks like marketplace revenue.Void if duplicated, or re-enter as Transfer if it was a payout.
Duplicate riskSimilar amount/vendor/date already exists.Confirm which entry is correct and void duplicates.

Review routine

  1. Open the selected month.
  2. Check the Review KPI count.
  3. Open Review Queue and resolve each issue.
  4. Refresh and confirm the Review KPI dropped.
  5. Export only after critical issues are resolved.
13. Export and Month-End Close

Preparing data for finance review

Use Export when you need a CSV of manual accounting transactions for the selected month or custom range. The export is intended for reconciliation, review, or handoff to the person preparing final accounting records.

Export steps

1
Select the month or custom period.
2
Click Refresh so the screen is current.
3
Resolve critical review items and missing receipt items.
4
Click Export.
5
Open the CSV and compare against bank statements, cash counts, wallet payout history, and supplier invoices.

Monthly close checklist

  • All known supplier invoices are entered as bills.
  • All paid bills are marked paid or partially paid.
  • All daily expenses have category, vendor, brand, and channel.
  • Marketplace payouts are transfers, not manual income.
  • Offline/manual income has source and reference.
  • Receipt status is accurate for every material entry.
  • Overdue bills are explained or resolved.
  • Review Queue has no critical items.
  • Safe Cash is reviewed before discretionary spending.
  • CSV export is saved for the period.
14. Best Practices

Rules that keep Accounting usable

Enter daily, not weekly

Daily entry reduces forgotten vendors, missing receipts, and incorrect dates.

Use bills for unpaid invoices

If we owe money but have not paid, create a bill. Do not wait until payment day.

Do not double-count payouts

Marketplace payout is a transfer from wallet to bank, not new revenue.

Attach evidence early

Missing receipts create review work and slow month close.

Void, do not delete

Voiding keeps a reason and preserves audit history.

Keep categories boring

Use consistent categories. Do not create vague vendor names or broad catch-all notes.

Common mistakes

MistakeResultCorrect approach
Entering marketplace payout as Manual Money InRevenue/cash is double-counted.Use Transfer from marketplace wallet to bank.
Entering unpaid invoice as Expense PaidCash looks lower before payment happens.Use Bill Received, then Pay Bill later.
Skipping vendor/sourceInsights become useless.Enter the vendor, payee, source, or customer.
Leaving receipt missingReview Queue stays open.Attach URL or mark Not required.
Using wrong dateMonth totals become inaccurate.Use actual movement date or invoice date.

Escalate when

  • Cash Available does not match the bank/cash reality after refresh.
  • Marketplace Outstanding looks materially wrong.
  • Safe Cash is negative or unexpectedly low.
  • Overdue bills are growing.
  • Review Queue contains items you cannot resolve confidently.
